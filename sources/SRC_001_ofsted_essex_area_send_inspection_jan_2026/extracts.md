Source location:

```text
Page 1, inspection outcome
```

Extract or paraphrase:


## Extract 004: Unequal access to health services

Source location:

```text
Page 2, health access findings
```

Extract or paraphrase:

```text
The report says children and young people with SEND do not have equal access to all health services, and that access depends too much on where they live. It identifies variable community children's nursing, variable waits for mental health support and long, unequal waits for neurodevelopmental assessments.
```

Potential relevance:

- user need: need to access health support consistently regardless of where they live
- civic need: need for equitable access to health services
- pain point: postcode variation in access to SEND-related health support
- locality context: Essex
- service context: SEND, health, mental health, neurodevelopmental assessment

Handling notes:

```text
Candidate evidence for locality and equity sensemaking. Do not over-generalise beyond the source without comparison to other areas.
```

## Extract 005: Preparation for adulthood gaps

Source location:

```text
Page 3, preparation for adulthood findings
```

```text
The report says some children and young people receive positive preparation-for-adulthood support, but there are gaps in children's health support as they become adults.
```
